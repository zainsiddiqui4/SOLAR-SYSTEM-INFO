The Eight Planets

In order of distance from the Sun. Click a planet in the sky above to jump straight to it.

Mercury

The Swift Planet

The smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. A year here lasts just 88 Earth days, but one day–night cycle takes 176 — so a day is longer than a year!

  • Distance58M km
  • Moons0
  • Day temp430°C

Venus

The Morning Star

The hottest planet in the solar system thanks to a runaway greenhouse effect. Its thick clouds of sulphuric acid trap heat like a giant oven.

  • Distance108M km
  • Moons0
  • Surface465°C

Earth

The Blue Marble

The only known world with life. About 71% of its surface is covered in liquid water, and its atmosphere shields us from harmful solar radiation.

  • Distance150M km
  • Moons1
  • Age4.5B yrs

Mars

The Red Planet

Rusty iron dust gives Mars its colour. It hosts Olympus Mons — the tallest volcano in the solar system, nearly three times the height of Mount Everest.

  • Distance228M km
  • Moons2
  • Gravity38% of Earth

Jupiter

The Giant

So massive that all other planets could fit inside it — twice over. Its Great Red Spot is a storm bigger than Earth that has raged for centuries.

  • Distance778M km
  • Moons95
  • Day length10 hrs

Saturn

The Ringed Jewel

Its famous rings are made of billions of chunks of ice and rock, some as small as dust and some as big as houses. Saturn is so light it could float on water.

  • Distance1.4B km
  • Moons146
  • Ring span282,000 km

Uranus

The Sideways World

Uranus rolls around the Sun tipped over on its side, probably after a giant collision long ago. Each pole gets 42 years of sunlight, then 42 years of darkness.

  • Distance2.9B km
  • Moons28
  • Tilt98°

Neptune

The Windy Blue

The farthest planet, discovered by mathematics before it was ever seen through a telescope. Its winds scream at over 2,000 km/h — the fastest anywhere.

  • Distance4.5B km
  • Moons16
  • Year165 Earth yrs
